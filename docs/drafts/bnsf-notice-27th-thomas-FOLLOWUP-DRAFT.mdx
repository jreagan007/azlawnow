This is a short note from the Editor-in-Chief desk.

On May 12, 2026, AZ Law Now's legal team, led by Brandon Millam, J.D., accepted Sonja Celius's case. The firm is counsel of record.

AZ Law Now's editorial team had already published reporting on the 27th Avenue and Thomas Road grade-crossing corridor in west Phoenix before the case acceptance. That investigation continues to live at the published URL, with the federal-record findings updated as new Form 6180.57 filings appear. The piece reports from primary public records and now carries a counsel-of-record disclaimer footer.

The editorial side and the legal side of AZ Law Now operate as separate functions inside the firm. That distinction matters and it is the reason this note exists.

## What changes editorially

We continue to report from public records. The Federal Railroad Administration Office of Safety Analysis files are public. The City of Phoenix project page is public. The state and federal regulatory framework is public. Our reporting cites those records and nothing else. We have filed a five-request public-records packet to the Phoenix Police Department, the Arizona Department of Transportation Rail Section, the City of Phoenix Streets Transportation Department, the Arizona Attorney General Consumer Protection Section, and the Federal Railroad Administration. Those records, when returned, will appear in the editorial under the same primary-source discipline.

## What does not change

The legal work is handled by Brandon Millam, J.D. and the AZ Law Now legal team. The editorial team does not see attorney-client communications. The editorial team does not advance litigation theory in its reporting. The piece on the corridor does not state legal conclusions; it states what the public records say. Under Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct ER 3.6, the piece is editorial commentary on public records and does not carry a substantial likelihood of materially prejudicing the underlying proceeding.

## What we are not doing

We are not contacting BNSF Railway Company on the editorial side. Under ER 4.2, any contact with represented parties on the subject of the representation runs through the litigation side via formal discovery channels, not through editorial. BNSF Public Affairs was struck from the press outreach plan when the case was accepted.

## The interview offer

Journalists covering Sonja Celius's case, the 27th Avenue and Thomas Road grade-crossing corridor, or AZ rail-safety policy more broadly can interview AZ Law Now.

- **Brandon Millam, J.D.**, Legal Editor and counsel of record. He speaks to the firm's approach to grade-crossing safety litigation, the federal regulatory framework, the public-records framework on AZ railroad corridors, and the structural questions about two-quadrant gate retrofits at urban arterial crossings. He does not speak to the merits of the underlying case, the litigation strategy, the value of the claim, or any client communication. Brandon is licensed in Arizona.
- **Brendan Franks**, Editor-in-Chief. He speaks to the public records that AZ Law Now has published on the corridor, the Form 6180.57 dataset, the methodology behind the findings, and the records-request packet now in flight. He does not speak to client representation, the case, or any matter inside the legal team's work.

Interview availability Tuesday through Thursday, 9 AM to 5 PM Phoenix time. Press requests to `brendan@azlawnow.com` (editorial) or `brandon@azlawnow.com` (legal).

## What we will continue to publish

The Form 6180.57 dataset on the corridor as new BNSF filings appear. The City of Phoenix Capital Improvement Program records when they return under ARS 39-121. The ADOT Rail Section correspondence when it returns. The FRA Office of Railroad Safety inspector defect reports when they return under FOIA. The corridor-comparison work showing whether the structural failure modes BNSF documented at Thomas Road in 2025 appear at other AZ two-quadrant retrofit sites. The federal regulatory framework analysis on supplementary safety measures under 49 CFR Part 222.

Every piece will carry the counsel-of-record disclaimer for the duration of the representation. Every piece will cite primary sources. Every piece will report what the records say.

## A note on transparency

Some readers will read this note and wonder why we are publishing it at all. The short answer: we believe the editorial work on the 27th Avenue corridor matters and we believe AZ readers deserve full disclosure of the firm's posture as the case proceeds. The longer answer is in our editorial standards under the Arizona Rules of Professional Conduct: where a firm publishes on a matter and accepts representation on the same matter, the disclosure standard is to put the relationship on the record. This note does that.

Sonja Celius and her family are in our thoughts. The corridor matters. The editorial continues.
