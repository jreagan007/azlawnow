## 2. ER 3.6 — Pre-Trial Publicity / Extrajudicial Statements

**Rule (paraphrased):** a lawyer participating in or who has participated in the investigation or litigation of a matter shall not make an extrajudicial statement that the lawyer knows or reasonably should know will be disseminated by means of public communication and will have a substantial likelihood of materially prejudicing an adjudicative proceeding.

**Question for Brandon:**

Does the existing MDX, or the proposed Option-1 lede rewrite, qualify as an extrajudicial statement under ER 3.6 that has a substantial likelihood of materially prejudicing the Celius adjudicative proceeding?

The piece:
- Names BNSF as the party that operated the crossing
- Names the structural defect (two-quadrant gate, no channelizing medians, no four-quadrant gates)
- Quotes BNSF's own Form 6180.57 narratives identifying the failure modes
- Implies the cause of the strike was the structural defect

ER 3.6(c) permits a lawyer to state without elaboration:
1. The claim, offense, or defense and, except when prohibited by law, the identity of the persons involved
2. Information contained in a public record
3. That an investigation of the matter is in progress
4. The scheduling or result of any step in litigation
5. A request for assistance in obtaining evidence
6. A warning of danger concerning the behavior of a person involved (when reason to believe substantial likelihood of substantial harm)

The piece, read narrowly, is "information contained in a public record" (FRA Form 6180.57 is a public record per 49 USC 20901(e); the FRA inventory is public; the City of Phoenix project description is public). That's likely a defense if challenged.

However, the framing (corporate-notice timeline, mitigations-not-installed argument, BNSF's-own-words-document-the-failure-mode) goes beyond "stating the public record." It assembles the public record into a litigation theory.

The conservative path: have Brandon redact or generalize the framing so the piece reports the public record without assembling it into a causation theory. The aggressive path: publish the piece as-is and assert ER 3.6(c)(2) protection.

**Recommendation pending Brandon:** lean conservative. The litigation theory is what discovery and trial are for. The journalism is what the public record states. Frame the piece around the public-record statements, not the structural-defect conclusion.

## 4. Conflict of Interest / Material Limitation

**Rule (paraphrased):** a lawyer shall not represent a client if the representation involves a concurrent conflict of interest. A concurrent conflict exists if there is a significant risk that the representation of one client will be materially limited by the lawyer's responsibilities to another client, a former client, or a third person, or by a personal interest of the lawyer.

**Question for Brandon:**

The firm's editorial brand (AZ Law Now as independent data journalism) and the firm's litigation practice (counsel of record on personal injury cases) intersect at this investigation. Is there a material-limitation conflict?
