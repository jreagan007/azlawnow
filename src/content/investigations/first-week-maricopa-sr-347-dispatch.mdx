We haven't even finished unpacking the boxes.

We got to Maricopa less than a week ago. Same reason a lot of people pick this town. The community feel. The desert horizon. The chance to build something in a city that's still figuring out what it wants to be.

What I wasn't fully ready for was SR-347.

## Four Days In

Thursday, January 15. I was in the standstill traffic backed up behind the crash near Cement Plant Road. As a newcomer, my first instinct was the same as anyone's. Late. Stuck. No clue what the back roads are yet.

Then the news broke.

A fatal crash. Someone's Thursday morning became the worst day of their family's life.

The frustration turned into something heavier. This road isn't a commute we complain about. It's a shared vulnerability that every Maricopa resident navigates twice a day.

What I noticed next was what the town did. Checking on each other. Sharing rerouting info. Strangers coordinating in a way that said this has happened before and we know the drill.

That's the town I moved to. Resilient when the only lifeline shuts down.

## Who I Am

Let me step out of the traffic and introduce myself properly.

My name is [Brendan Franks](/about/#brendan-franks). I moved here to serve Maricopa residents. Not a Phoenix firm with a phone number and a PO box in town. The office is here. I live here. My family shops here. The cases we take come from this corridor and the communities on either end of it.

The ribbon-cutting is in a few weeks. We'll be open before it. I'll be in the park on Sunday mornings and at the GMBA mixers and at whatever the next community event turns out to be.

Seeing those flashing lights on the 15th made the job feel different. Being a good neighbor in Maricopa starts before you pull into your driveway. It starts on the highway. Patience. Defensive driving. Looking out for the motorcyclists and the school buses and the families sharing two lanes with you.

## What I'm Asking

I'm learning fast that Waze and Google Maps don't tell the whole story of SR-347.

You can't code for local knowledge.

So as we settle in, I want to learn from the veterans. The people who drive this corridor every morning and have for years.

What are the safety habits locals swear by?

When the road shuts down like it did on the 15th, what's your routing strategy?

For a newcomer, what's the single most important defensive driving tip for the bottleneck?

## Going Forward

I've since [pulled the crash data](/investigations/sr-347-crash-corridor/) for the full SR-347 corridor. The numbers are worse than the reputation. The [interchange at I-10](/investigations/sr-347-i-10-interchange/) is where the geometry and the volume collide. I'll keep writing about this road because writing about it's part of being here.

My heart goes out to the family affected by the crash on the 15th. It was a tragic reminder for all of us to slow down and pay attention.

Looking forward to meeting more of you in person. Hopefully under better circumstances than a traffic jam.

Brendan Franks
