## What it means

A doctrine that holds property owners liable for injuries to children attracted onto the property by a dangerous condition (pool, abandoned car, machinery), even if the children were technically trespassing.

## Arizona context

Arizona recognizes the attractive nuisance doctrine. Property owners must take reasonable steps to protect children from foreseeable dangers, especially when the condition is one that would attract young children.
