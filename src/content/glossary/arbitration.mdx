## What it means

A dispute resolution process where a neutral arbitrator hears evidence and issues a binding decision. Faster and less formal than trial, but limits appeal rights.

## Arizona context

Arizona's Rules of Civil Procedure require mandatory arbitration for cases under a certain dollar threshold (varies by county). Either party can appeal the arbitration result and request a new trial.
