Slip and fall injuries are easy to dismiss. Until they happen to you. A wet floor in a grocery store, a broken step at an apartment complex, an icy patch in a parking lot, a cracked sidewalk outside a strip mall. One moment you're walking normally. The next you're on the ground with a fractured hip, a broken wrist, or a head injury that changes everything.

## The Notice Requirement

Arizona slip and fall cases hinge on a legal concept called notice. You must show that the property owner knew about the dangerous condition, or should have known about it through reasonable inspection, and failed to fix it or warn visitors.

**Actual notice** means the property owner knew about the hazard. Maybe someone reported a spill. Maybe a maintenance request was filed about a broken handrail. Maybe the condition had existed for so long that management couldn't plausibly deny knowledge.

**Constructive notice** means the hazard was so obvious or existed for so long that a reasonable property owner would have discovered it through regular inspections. A puddle that sat in a grocery aisle for two hours. A pothole in a parking lot that had been there for months. Crumbling steps that had deteriorated over years.

The insurance company will argue the property owner didn't know. That's why evidence matters. Surveillance footage, maintenance logs, inspection schedules, and prior incident reports all help establish notice.

## Common Dangerous Conditions

**Wet floors:** spills, leaks, recently mopped surfaces, rain tracked inside from entrances. Grocery stores, restaurants, and hospitals see these hazards constantly.

**Uneven surfaces:** cracked sidewalks, raised concrete joints, potholes in parking lots, uneven transitions between flooring types.

**Poor lighting:** stairwells, parking garages, and walkways without adequate illumination make it impossible to see hazards.

**Missing or broken handrails:** stairs and ramps without functional handrails are especially dangerous for older adults and anyone with mobility limitations.

**Cluttered walkways:** merchandise, cords, boxes, or debris blocking paths of travel create tripping hazards.

## Comparative Negligence in Fall Cases

Arizona's pure comparative negligence system applies to slip and fall cases. The property owner's insurance company will argue you weren't paying attention, were wearing inappropriate footwear, or were in an area where you shouldn't have been. Their goal is to assign you as much fault as possible.

Even if they succeed in assigning you partial fault, you can still recover. A 25% fault finding means you collect 75% of your damages. We push back aggressively on comparative fault arguments because every percentage point directly affects your recovery.

## Why These Cases Are Worth Pursuing

Fall injuries can be devastating, especially for adults over 50. A hip fracture often requires surgery and months of rehabilitation. Many people never regain their previous level of mobility. Traumatic brain injuries from hitting a hard surface can cause permanent cognitive changes. Back injuries can become chronic pain conditions that limit activity for years.

The medical costs add up. Surgery, hospital stays, physical therapy, assistive devices, home modifications. Lost wages during recovery can strain families that are already dealing with medical bills. These are real, documented damages that Arizona law allows you to recover.

## Preserve the Evidence

Evidence in slip and fall cases disappears fast. The spill gets cleaned. The broken step gets repaired. The surveillance footage gets overwritten. If you've been hurt in a fall, take these steps immediately.

Photograph the hazardous condition, the surrounding area, and your injuries. Report the fall to the property owner and request a written incident report. Get names and contact information for any witnesses. Seek medical treatment. Then call (602) 654-0202 before the evidence disappears.
