You go to a doctor, a surgeon, a hospital because you need help. You trust them to know what they're doing. When that trust is violated, when a healthcare provider makes a mistake that causes serious harm, the consequences can reshape your entire life. A botched surgery. A missed diagnosis. A medication error. These aren't abstractions. They're real injuries with real, lasting impact.

## What the Law Requires

Medical malpractice isn't about a bad outcome. Medicine involves risk, and not every complication means someone made a mistake. The legal standard is whether the healthcare provider met the accepted standard of care for their specialty. Did they do what a reasonably competent provider would have done in the same situation?

Arizona requires expert testimony to answer that question. A qualified medical expert in the same or similar specialty must review the records, identify the standard of care, and explain how the defendant fell short. Without this expert opinion, the case can't proceed. We work with board-certified specialists across disciplines to evaluate every case we consider.

## The Discovery Rule

Arizona's statute of limitations for medical malpractice is two years, but the clock doesn't always start on the date of the procedure. The discovery rule says the deadline runs from the date you knew or reasonably should have known about the injury and its connection to the medical care you received.

This matters in cases involving retained surgical instruments, slow-developing complications, or misdiagnoses where the correct diagnosis comes months or years later. The discovery rule protects patients who couldn't have known something was wrong right away.

That said, don't wait. The sooner you contact an attorney, the better your chances of preserving evidence and identifying the right experts.

## No Damage Caps in Arizona

Many states cap non-economic damages in medical malpractice cases. Arizona doesn't. The Arizona Constitution expressly prohibits legislative caps on personal injury damages. That means juries can award whatever amount they determine is fair, with no artificial ceiling on pain and suffering, loss of enjoyment, or other non-economic damages.

This is significant. Severe malpractice injuries, like brain damage from an anesthesia error or paralysis from a surgical mistake, cause decades of suffering. Arizona's constitutional protection ensures those damages are fully compensable.

## Common Types of Medical Malpractice

**Surgical errors:** wrong-site surgery, retained instruments, nerve damage, and complications from procedures performed below the standard of care.

**Diagnostic failures:** missed cancers, undiagnosed infections, overlooked fractures, and conditions that worsen because the doctor didn't catch them early enough.

**Medication errors:** wrong drug, wrong dose, dangerous drug interactions, or failure to check for known allergies.

**Birth injuries:** oxygen deprivation, excessive force during delivery, failure to perform a timely C-section, and failure to monitor fetal distress.

**Hospital negligence:** understaffing, infection control failures, patient falls, inadequate monitoring, and premature discharge.
