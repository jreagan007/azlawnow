When facilities fall short of those standards and a child gets hurt, the facility bears responsibility.

## ADHS Licensing and Regulatory Standards

The Arizona Department of Health Services licenses and inspects childcare facilities under Title 9, Chapter 3 of the Arizona Administrative Code. These regulations aren't suggestions. They're legal requirements that every licensed facility must follow.

The rules cover staff qualifications and background checks, staff-to-child ratios by age group, physical facility safety standards, supervision requirements during all activities, medication administration protocols, emergency procedures and evacuation plans, and record-keeping obligations.

When a facility violates these standards and a child is injured as a result, the violation itself is powerful evidence of negligence. We obtain ADHS inspection records, complaint investigation files, and enforcement history for every facility we investigate.

## Staffing Ratios Matter

Arizona mandates minimum staff-to-child ratios based on the children's ages. These ratios exist because young children require constant supervision. An infant who chokes. A toddler who climbs unsafe equipment. A preschooler who wanders into a dangerous area. Adequate staffing prevents these situations.

When facilities operate below required ratios to save money, children get hurt. We compare the facility's actual staffing on the day of the incident against both the legal requirements and the facility's own policies. The gap between what the law requires and what the facility provided tells the story.

## Common Daycare Injuries

Children at daycare face risks that proper supervision and facility maintenance should prevent. Playground falls happen when equipment isn't maintained or when staff aren't watching. Choking incidents occur when young children eat without adequate supervision. Burns result from unsecured hot liquids or malfunctioning equipment. Near-drowning events happen at facilities with pools or water features that lack proper barriers and monitoring.

Abuse by staff members represents the most troubling category. Background check failures, inadequate hiring practices, and failure to investigate complaints all create conditions where abuse can occur. Arizona law holds the facility responsible when its negligence allowed a dangerous person access to children.

## Unlicensed Facilities

Arizona requires childcare providers to obtain an ADHS license. Unlicensed facilities operate outside the law, with no inspections, no required background checks, and no regulatory oversight. When a child is injured at an unlicensed facility, the operator's decision to bypass the licensing process is itself evidence of disregard for children's safety.

We've pursued claims against both licensed and unlicensed facilities. The legal theories differ, but the goal is the same: full compensation for the child's injuries and accountability for the people responsible.

## Building the Case

Daycare negligence cases require rapid evidence preservation. Facilities may alter records, erase surveillance footage, or coach staff on what to say. When you contact us, we move quickly to demand preservation of all records, video, and incident reports related to your child's injury.

We also pull the facility's complete ADHS history, looking for prior complaints, inspection failures, and corrective action plans. A pattern of problems strengthens the case that the facility knew about safety gaps and chose not to fix them.
