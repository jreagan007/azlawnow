## What Makes Arizona Car Crashes Different

Arizona operates under a pure comparative negligence system. That's a critical distinction. It means you can recover compensation even if you were partially at fault for the crash. Your award gets reduced by your percentage of responsibility, but it doesn't disappear.

Insurance companies know this. They'll try to assign as much fault to you as possible. Their adjusters are trained to ask leading questions, record statements, and find reasons to reduce your payout. Having an attorney on your side changes that dynamic.

## The Two-Year Deadline

Under ARS 12-542, you have two years from the date of the crash to file a personal injury claim in Arizona. That sounds like a long time. It isn't. Medical records take weeks to compile. Investigations require time. Expert witnesses need to review the evidence. Starting early gives your legal team room to build the strongest case possible.

If a family member died in the crash, the wrongful death statute (ARS 12-611) also carries a two-year deadline. These cases involve additional complexity around who can file and what damages are available.

## What Your Case Is Worth

There's no single answer. The value of a car crash case depends on your medical bills, lost income, the severity of your injuries, and how the crash affects your daily life going forward. Arizona law allows recovery for both economic damages (medical costs, lost wages, property damage) and non-economic damages (pain, suffering, loss of enjoyment).
