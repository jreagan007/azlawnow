Schools occupy a position of trust. Parents send their children to school every day expecting them to be safe. Arizona law reinforces that expectation through statutes that require school districts to hire qualified staff, maintain safe facilities, and protect students from foreseeable harm. When schools fail those obligations and a child is hurt, the district answers for it.

Brandon Millam and our team handle civil claims against school districts and individual school employees throughout Arizona. We navigate the governmental immunity framework and the strict notice deadlines that apply to these cases.

## ARS 15-341 and the School's Duty

Arizona Revised Statutes 15-341 outlines the governing board's responsibilities for every public school district. Those responsibilities include maintaining safe school buildings and grounds, establishing policies for student safety, conducting fingerprint-based background checks on all employees, and responding to reports of employee misconduct.

When a school district fails to follow these statutory requirements and a student is harmed, the failure becomes the foundation of a negligence claim. The district didn't just make a mistake. It broke the law.

## Governmental Immunity and Its Limits

Arizona's governmental immunity doctrine protects public entities from some lawsuits, but it doesn't create a blanket shield. School districts remain liable when their employees cause harm through negligent acts within the scope of employment. Districts also remain liable when they fail to comply with mandatory statutory duties.

The critical procedural requirement is the 180-day notice of claim under ARS 12-821.01. Before filing a lawsuit against a school district, families must serve a formal notice describing the claim, the injuries, and the compensation sought. The district has 60 days to respond. Missing the 180-day deadline eliminates the right to sue entirely.

This deadline catches many families off guard. Six months passes quickly when you're focused on your child's medical care and emotional recovery. Contact an attorney immediately so the notice gets filed on time.

## Types of School Abuse and Negligence

**Sexual abuse by staff:** Teachers, coaches, counselors, and support staff who sexually abuse students create liability for themselves and the district. The district is liable if it failed to screen the employee, ignored prior complaints, or created conditions that enabled the abuse. Arizona's extended statute of limitations allows survivors to file until age 30.

**Physical abuse:** Excessive physical discipline, corporal punishment, and physical assaults by staff members. Arizona prohibits corporal punishment in public schools. Any physical contact that causes injury is grounds for a claim.

**Negligent supervision:** Students injured during school hours because staff weren't monitoring them properly. Playground injuries, cafeteria incidents, and hallway altercations that the school should have prevented through adequate supervision.

**Failure to address bullying:** Schools that know about persistent bullying and fail to take reasonable steps to stop it can be liable for the resulting physical and psychological harm. Documentation of prior reports matters enormously in these cases.
