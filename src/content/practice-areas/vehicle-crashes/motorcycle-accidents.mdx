Arizona's year-round riding weather draws motorcyclists from across the country. It also means more bikes on the road, more interactions with distracted drivers, and more crashes. When a car turns left in front of a motorcycle or merges without checking a blind spot, the rider pays the price. There's no metal frame, no airbag, no crumple zone between you and the pavement.

## The Bias Problem

Here's something most attorneys won't tell you upfront: motorcycle riders face bias. Insurance adjusters, defense lawyers, and sometimes juries carry assumptions about motorcyclists. They assume you were speeding. They assume you were weaving through traffic. They assume you accepted the risk when you got on the bike.

That bias shows up in lower settlement offers and tougher negotiations. Overcoming it requires an attorney who understands the dynamics and knows how to reframe the conversation around the other driver's negligence, not your choice of vehicle.

## Arizona's Motorcycle Laws

Arizona doesn't require helmets for riders 18 and older. That's the law. But insurance companies treat it as an opportunity. They'll argue that your head injuries would've been less severe with a helmet, and they'll push to reduce your compensation based on that argument.

Lane splitting remains illegal in Arizona, unlike California. If you were filtering between lanes at the time of the crash, expect the defense to raise comparative negligence. Arizona's pure comparative negligence system means you can still recover, but your award gets reduced by your share of fault.

## Road Hazards Hit Riders Hardest

A pothole that a car rolls over without noticing can send a motorcycle airborne. Gravel on a curve, an oil slick at an intersection, a patch of uneven pavement where construction ended. These hazards are minor inconveniences for four-wheeled vehicles and life-threatening for riders.

West Valley roads are especially problematic. Rapid development in Buckeye, Goodyear, and Surprise means constant construction. Loose gravel, temporary surfaces, and poorly marked lane shifts create hazards that riders encounter daily along routes like MC 85, Yuma Road, and Estrella Parkway.

When a road defect causes a crash, the responsible government entity may be liable. These claims require a notice of claim filed within 180 days under ARS 12-821.01. Miss that deadline and you lose the right to sue.

## Injuries That Demand Serious Representation

Motorcycle crash injuries are typically more severe than car crash injuries. Road rash that requires skin grafts. Fractures that need multiple surgeries. Traumatic brain injuries that change everything. Spinal cord damage that affects mobility for life.

The medical costs add up quickly. Surgery, hospital stays, physical therapy, occupational therapy, assistive devices, home modifications. We work with medical experts to project the full cost of your recovery, including care you'll need years from now.

Call 911. Get medical attention even if the adrenaline makes you feel okay. Take photos of the scene, the vehicles, the road conditions, and your injuries. Get witness information. Don't discuss fault with anyone at the scene or with any insurance adjuster.
