An 80,000-pound commercial truck doesn't stop like a car. It doesn't handle like a car. And when it collides with a passenger vehicle, the damage isn't comparable. The physics are brutal: a fully loaded semi traveling at highway speed carries the force of a small building hitting your vehicle. Survivors often face months or years of recovery. Some injuries are permanent.

## Federal Regulations Change Everything

Truck crash cases aren't just bigger car crash cases. They're fundamentally different. The Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration regulates every aspect of commercial trucking: driver qualifications, hours of service, vehicle maintenance, cargo loading, and insurance minimums.

When a crash happens, those regulations become your leverage. Did the driver exceed hours-of-service limits? Was the truck properly maintained? Were pre-trip inspections documented? Did the carrier hire a driver with a history of violations? Every FMCSA regulation that was broken strengthens your case.

Most personal injury attorneys don't handle these cases regularly. We do. We know where to look, what to request, and how to interpret the data.

## Arizona's Truck Crash Corridors

The I-10 between Phoenix and Tucson is one of the heaviest freight corridors in the western US. Commercial trucks share this highway with commuters, and the combination of high speeds, construction zones, and driver fatigue creates dangerous conditions daily.

In the West Valley, trucks traveling through Buckeye and Goodyear on I-10 and along MC 85 frequently interact with local traffic. The rapid growth in these communities means more passenger vehicles sharing roads designed for different traffic volumes. Crashes at intersections where commercial routes meet residential areas are increasingly common.

## The Evidence Disappears Fast

Trucking companies know the stakes. Their insurance carriers can put investigators on the scene within hours of a crash. Meanwhile, critical evidence has a short shelf life. Electronic logging devices overwrite data. Black box recordings get replaced. Drug and alcohol testing must happen within specific windows.

That's why timing matters. When you call us after a truck crash, one of our first actions is sending a spoliation letter to the trucking company demanding they preserve all evidence related to the crash. This legal notice prevents them from "routinely" destroying records that could prove your case.

## Multiple Parties, Multiple Policies

A car crash typically involves two drivers and two insurance policies. A truck crash can involve the driver, the trucking company, the freight broker, the cargo loading company, and the vehicle manufacturer. Each may carry separate insurance. Each may share liability.

We investigate every party and every policy. More responsible parties means more available coverage for your recovery. Federal minimum insurance requirements start at $750,000 for most commercial carriers and go up to $5 million for hazardous materials haulers.

## Injuries That Change Lives

Truck crash injuries tend to be severe: traumatic brain injuries, spinal cord damage, multiple fractures, internal organ damage, and amputations. These injuries require years of medical care and rehabilitation. Some require lifetime assistance. The costs are staggering.
