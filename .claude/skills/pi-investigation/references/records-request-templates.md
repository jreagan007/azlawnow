# ARS 39-121 Public Records Request Templates

> AZ Law Now investigations use these templates to request government records for
> data-journalism pieces under Brendan Franks' byline. All requests are made under
> ARS 39-121 (Arizona public records law). The journalism/public-interest fee waiver
> language is included in each template. Track every request in
> `docs/investigations/<slug>/records-log.md`.
>
> Statutory window: ARS 39-121.01(D) requires the agency to respond within **10
> business days** (acknowledge the request; production timeline may differ for large
> sets). If no response is received within 10 business days, follow up in writing and
> cc the Arizona Attorney General's Office public records compliance contact.
>
> Fee waiver: ARS 39-121.01(D)(3) permits fee waiver when disclosure is "primarily in
> the public interest." Include the journalism/public-interest waiver paragraph in every
> request. If the agency denies the waiver, log the denial and evaluate whether to pay
> or escalate.

## Template 1 — ADOT Crash Records (Statewide / Corridor-Specific)

**To:** ADOT Public Records Office
**Email:** publicrecords@azdot.gov
**Subject:** ARS 39-121 Public Records Request — [Route/Corridor] Crash Data

---

Dear ADOT Public Records Office,

Pursuant to Arizona Revised Statutes Section 39-121, I am requesting the following
public records on behalf of AZ Law Now, a digital publication covering Arizona
personal-injury law and public-safety accountability.

**Records requested:**

1. All crash-level records for [ROUTE NAME / CORRIDOR DESCRIPTION, e.g., "State Route
   347 between the Gila River bridge and Maricopa Road interchange"] for calendar
   years [YYYY]–[YYYY], including: date, time, mile post, manner of collision, severity
   (fatal/incapacitating/non-incapacitating/possible/property-damage-only), roadway
   conditions, lighting conditions, contributing circumstances, number of vehicles
   involved, and crash report number.

2. Any ADOT-authored or ADOT-commissioned corridor safety study, safety action plan,
   or hazard-elimination program report for [ROUTE NAME / CORRIDOR] issued between
   [YYYY] and present.

3. Any ADOT Capital Improvement Program line items or project entries for [ROUTE NAME
   / CORRIDOR] for fiscal years [YYYY]–[YYYY], including project type, scheduled
   construction date, and funding source.

4. Any written correspondence between ADOT and the Federal Highway Administration
   regarding [ROUTE NAME / CORRIDOR] safety conditions, including any Section 148
   Highway Safety Improvement Program (HSIP) project applications, approvals, or
   audit findings.

**Format requested:** Electronic records in PDF, CSV, or Excel format, whichever is
already maintained. I do not require printed production.

**Fee waiver request:** I request a waiver of all fees associated with this request
pursuant to ARS 39-121.01(D)(3). AZ Law Now is a public-interest publication whose
investigative journalism concerning Arizona transportation safety is intended to inform
the public, not for commercial gain. Disclosure of these records is primarily in the
public interest. If the agency declines to waive fees entirely, please provide a written
fee estimate before producing records.

**Acknowledgment:** Please acknowledge receipt of this request within 10 business days
as required by ARS 39-121.01(D).

If you require clarification or have questions, please contact me at [EMAIL] or
[PHONE].

## Records log format

Every filed request must be logged in `docs/investigations/<slug>/records-log.md`
using this format:

```markdown
## Request Log — <Investigation Slug>

| Date filed | Agency | Contact | Template used | Response due | Response received | Documents received | Fee waiver granted |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| YYYY-MM-DD | ADOT Public Records | publicrecords@azdot.gov | Template 1 | 10 BD from YYYY-MM-DD | YYYY-MM-DD | Yes / No / Partial | Yes / No / Partial |
```

If the agency misses the 10-business-day acknowledgment window, log that date and send
a written follow-up citing ARS 39-121.01(D). If production is unreasonably delayed
beyond 30 calendar days, evaluate escalation to the Arizona Attorney General's public
records compliance process.

---

## Fee waiver escalation language

If an agency denies the journalism/public-interest fee waiver, include this paragraph
in the follow-up:

> AZ Law Now respectfully renews its fee waiver request. The requested records concern
> [government action / public safety condition] that is a matter of legitimate public
> concern under ARS 39-121.01(D)(3). The disclosure is sought for the purpose of
> informing the public through published investigative journalism, not for commercial
> resale of the records themselves. Denial of the fee waiver in these circumstances is
> inconsistent with the Arizona public records law's purpose of promoting governmental
> accountability. If you decline to reconsider, please provide the written basis for
> the denial so that AZ Law Now may evaluate its options under ARS 39-121.02.
