**Phone:** 602-262-1885
**Statute:** ARS 39-121, Arizona Public Records Law

**Scope of request:**

I am requesting all Phoenix Police Department calls for service, dispatch records, incident reports, and field-interview cards related to the following geographic area for the time period **June 1, 2024 through April 30, 2026** (the period beginning at completion of the railroad-crossing gate retrofit by BNSF Railway Company at 27th Avenue and Thomas Road in west Phoenix, and ending one week after the April 25, 2026 incident involving a pedestrian struck by a BNSF train at 27th Avenue):

- Intersection of North 27th Avenue and West Thomas Road, Phoenix, AZ 85017
- Intersection of North 27th Avenue and West Hadley Street, Phoenix, AZ
- Intersection of North 27th Avenue and West Garfield Street, Phoenix, AZ
- All locations within 1,000 feet of the BNSF Phoenix Subdivision main track, mileposts 189.580 through 189.615
- All locations within 200 feet of DOT grade crossing inventory numbers 025430G (27th Avenue) and 025617C (Thomas Road)

**Specific record types requested:**

1. CAD (Computer Aided Dispatch) records showing date, time, address, dispatch code, and disposition for every call
2. RMS (Records Management System) incident reports tied to those CAD calls
3. Field-interview cards generated within the geographic scope
4. Any train-strike, pedestrian-on-track, "person on tracks," "suicidal individual," or rail-related call dispositions
5. Any traffic-related calls indicating gate-arm failures, vehicle-stuck-on-tracks, or train-blocked-crossing complaints from citizens
6. Any Phoenix PD assistance-render calls coordinated with BNSF Railway Police or with the Federal Railroad Administration

**Format:** electronic, CSV preferred for CAD; PDF acceptable for incident reports. Redact PII per ARS 39-121.01(D) (victim addresses, witness names where the records relate to ongoing investigations).

**Fee waiver request:** under ARS 39-121.01(D)(2), requesting fee waiver as a member of the news media. AZ Law Now publishes independent journalism on Arizona public safety and civil accountability. ## What this packet does NOT request

- BNSF internal correspondence, dispatcher logs, engineer event recordings, locomotive cab video, or maintenance records. These are obtainable only through litigation discovery, are not subject to public-records statutes, and BNSF will refuse on trade-secret or operational-security grounds even in informal request.
- C3RS (Confidential Close Call Reporting System) records. These are explicitly protected under 49 USC 20118 and 49 CFR 232 from public disclosure. C3RS is not a public dataset.
- Personally identifying information (PII) on individual citizen complainants, victims, or witnesses. The journalism here is about institutional conduct, not individual identities. Redaction is welcome.
