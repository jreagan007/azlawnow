## Body

Hi Angel,

Your People piece on Sonja Celius surfaced a story that has a federal record behind it. I pulled the records this week.

Four months before Sonja was struck at 27th Avenue on April 25, BNSF Railway Company filed a Form 6180.57 accident report with the Federal Railroad Administration at the twin Thomas Road crossing fifty feet north. The narrative, written by BNSF, reads: "THE AUTO CAME UP AGAINST THE DIRECTION OF TRAFFIC FLOW NOT HAVING GATES." One passenger was injured in that December 15, 2025 incident. Two earlier BNSF filings at the same crossing in 2025 use the phrase "AUTO PRECEDED GATES." All three are post-gate. The gates went in June 2024.

The federal mitigations specified by 49 CFR Part 222 (channelizing medians, four-quadrant gates, supplementary safety measures) were not installed in the June 2024 scope. The FRA inventory revised July 18, 2025 confirms this. The City of Phoenix is the project owner; BNSF is the operator.

Methodology: per-crossing FRA Office of Safety Analysis pulls for both DOT crossings, with pre-gate vs post-gate verified via the warning-code field on each filing. The PDFs and extracted text are saved and shareable.

For a People follow-on, the angle the data supports:

- BNSF wrote it down. Three times. The structural failure mode the gates were supposed to fix is documented in the company's own filings.
- A pedestrian was struck 131 days after the third filing.
- The City of Phoenix project page describes the scope; the federal record documents what was not in that scope.

A note for full transparency: AZ Law Now signed Sonja Celius's case on May 12. I am the Editor-in-Chief, not counsel. The records work is dual-purpose and the editorial is independent. I am writing as the data desk, not as plaintiff press.

The federal records cited above are public. Free to use with credit. Happy to send the dataset, the BNSF narrative PDFs, or a fifteen-minute walkthrough if useful for a People follow-on.
