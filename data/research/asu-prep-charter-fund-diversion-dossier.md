## EXECUTIVE VERIFICATION SUMMARY

**THE $31M SHELL COMPANY HOOK IS UNSUPPORTED.** No primary-source document (AZ Auditor General report, ASBCS adverse action, AG complaint, court filing, SEC filing, IRS revocation) anywhere in the public record substantiates the claim that ASU Preparatory Academy diverted $31 million to a shell company since 2020. The hook as written cannot ship.

**What the record DOES show:**

1. ASU Prep's most recent 990 (FY ending June 2024) reports a $5.46M operating loss on $82.4M revenue. (HIGH)
2. The FY2021 audited consolidated financial statements explicitly disclose a related-party transaction. ASU Prep leases a school-site building from a related party under an agreement running through September 2032. (HIGH)
3. ASU Prep received $4.74M in federal awards in FY2021 alone, including $994,691 in ESSER COVID-relief funds and $1,584,899 in Coronavirus Relief Fund pass-through. (HIGH)
4. The FY2021 single audit's Schedule of Findings and Questioned Costs reports "None reported" for both financial statement findings and federal award findings. The auditor issued an unmodified (clean) opinion. (HIGH)
5. ASU Prep is a separate Arizona nonprofit corporation and 501(c)(3), EIN 26-0664313, formerly named University Public Schools Inc., renamed in 2013. It's affiliated with but not legally part of Arizona State University. (HIGH)

**Recommended editorial pivot:** The verifiable angle is "related-party real-estate leasing inside Arizona's largest university-affiliated charter network" plus "ASU Prep is operating at a $5.4M deficit despite $80M in contributions." That's an editorial story that survives legal review. The "shell company diversion of $31M" framing does not.

**What the FY2021 audit does disclose (HIGH confidence, primary source):**

> "ASU Prep leases a building used for a school site from a related party (see Note 9) under an agreement expiring September 2032, which is classified as an operating lease."

Source: ASU Preparatory Academy Consolidated Financial Statements, Year Ended June 30, 2021, p.11 of audit, Note 6 Operating Leases. URL: https://www.ade.az.gov/sfsinbound/GeneralUpload/183788.pdf

**The related-party identity is referenced as Note 9 in the audit but the page text returned does not name the counterparty.** That detail requires the full PDF or a follow-up site audit on the specific page. Without naming the counterparty from a primary source, it isn't legally safe to call it a "shell company."
