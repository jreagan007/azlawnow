## 1. EXECUTIVE VERIFICATION SUMMARY

**Anchor finding (HIGH confidence, primary-source verified):**
On March 4, 2026, the Arizona Corporation Commission voted **3 to 2** to approve a **22% water rate increase and a 154% wastewater rate increase** for the roughly **1,900 customers** of Picacho Water Company and Picacho Sewer Company, the regulated utilities that serve the Robson Ranch retirement community south of Casa Grande in Eloy. The combined average monthly bill jumps **$76.63** (water plus sewer). The utilities are now owned by JW Water Holdings, which the Netherlands-based private equity firm CVC DIF acquired in November 2024. AG Kris Mayes filed a rehearing application on March 31, 2026, demanding the Commission subpoena the JW Water purchase records and reconsider the case under the Arizona Constitution's "just and reasonable" rate standard. (HIGH, AZ Capitol Times, KJZZ, AG press release, ACC March 4 open-meeting highlights, AZ Free News.)

## 2. PICACHO WATER + PICACHO SEWER HISTORY

- **Picacho Water Company** and **Picacho Sewer Company** are the regulated water and wastewater utilities serving the Robson Ranch retirement community in Eloy. (HIGH, JW Water FAQ, Capitol Times.)
- **Last rate cases:** Picacho Water last raised rates in **1998**. Picacho Sewer last raised rates in **1999**. (HIGH, search results from `azcapitoltimes.com` reporting "had not filed a rate increase since 1998 and 1999, respectively.")
- **Why rates stayed flat for 27 years:** Robson Companies, the master-planned-community developer that built and operated the utilities, absorbed cost increases internally instead of filing rate cases. AG Mayes' release frames this as the previous owner having "held rates artificially low for 27 years." (HIGH, AG release; KJZZ resident reporting.)
- **Rates immediately before the March 4, 2026 vote:** Water averaged about $30.01/month, sewer averaged about $42.00/month for a typical residential user. (HIGH, ACC March 4, 2026 open-meeting highlights.)
- **Service area:** Robson Ranch, the 55-and-older community located on the south side of Casa Grande inside Eloy city limits. (HIGH, Capitol Times, KJZZ.)

## 8. STATUTORY FRAME

- **Arizona Constitution Article XV:** Creates the elected Arizona Corporation Commission and gives it exclusive ratemaking authority over public service corporations. Section 3 requires the Commission to set "just and reasonable" rates. This is the constitutional standard Mayes invokes in her rehearing application.
- **A.R.S. Title 40 (Public Utilities and Carriers):** Codifies ACC ratemaking procedure.
  - **A.R.S. section 40-250**, Requires utilities to file rate schedules with the Commission.
  - **A.R.S. section 40-252**, Authorizes the ACC to investigate, modify, or rescind any prior order, which is the statutory hook for a rehearing.
  - **A.R.S. section 40-253**, Allows any party of record (including the Attorney General appearing on behalf of ratepayers) to apply for a rehearing within 20 days of a Commission decision. The ACC has 20 days to grant the rehearing or it is deemed denied.
  - **A.R.S. section 40-254**, Provides the Superior Court appeal pathway after rehearing is denied. This is the next escalation point if the ACC declines Mayes' rehearing.
- **Federal Clean Water Act (33 U.S.C. section 1251 et seq.):** Picacho Sewer is a regulated wastewater system but the rate question is purely a state ratemaking issue. CWA is not a primary frame here.
- **Pre-pub action:** Confirm the exact A.R.S. section 40-253 timing window and whether Mayes filed within 20 days of the March 4 vote. March 31 is 27 days out, suggesting the March 4 decision may not have been the final order; the formal Decision is likely entered on a later date. This matters for the rehearing window calculation. (FLAG.)

## 9. COMPARABLE PE-OWNED-UTILITY RATE-HIKE CASES (NATIONAL CONTEXT)

- **BlackRock Global Infrastructure Partners + Canada Pension Plan acquisition of ALLETE / Minnesota Power (2025):** $6.2B deal opposed by consumer groups citing rate-hike risk. Approved by Minnesota PUC December 2025 with conditions. (Public source: Jacobin, "Private Equity Is Coming for Public Utilities," Aug 2025.)
- **BlackRock + EQT consortium $10.7B bid for AES Corporation (announced 2026):** Active deal; consumer-protection opposition still in PUC review. (PEStakeholder.org.)
- **Macquarie / Southern Water UK:** A long-running cautionary tale. Macquarie's 2007 to 2017 ownership of Thames Water is the most-cited example globally of PE infrastructure-extraction harming ratepayers. Bills rose, infrastructure decayed, and Thames Water sits in financial distress. Southern Water needed a 550 million GBP Macquarie equity injection in 2025 to maintain operations. (Bloomberg, Infrastructure Investor.)
- **National pattern (NCLC framing):** As private equity moves into community water systems, the firms seek a shorter time horizon for expected profits and have advocated for "fair value" acquisition legislation and Distribution System Improvement Charge (DSIC) mechanisms that shift costs to ratepayers. (NCLC, "WAAT: Equitable Water Rates.")
- **What Picacho is:** the same playbook landing on a 1,900-customer Arizona retirement community. The CVC press release language ("stable long-term cash flows," "additional growth and long-term value creation") tracks the exact pattern NCLC and Energy and Policy Institute have flagged.
