## 1. DEA enforcement framework

**HIGH confidence on framework, citations to primary statutes:**

- Controlled Substances Act, 21 U.S.C. 801 et seq. governs the federal scheduling and registration regime.
- DEA registration revocation procedures run through 21 C.F.R. 1301.36 and the Administrative Procedure Act, with decisions published in the Federal Register as "Decision and Order" entries.
- An **Immediate Suspension Order** under 21 U.S.C. 824(d) is the DEA's emergency tool when continued registration creates an imminent danger to public health.
- An **Order to Show Cause** under 21 U.S.C. 824(c) is the standard pre-revocation administrative pleading.
- DOJ Civil Division uses the **False Claims Act, 31 U.S.C. 3729**, often through qui tam relators, to recover federal dollars on Medicare and Medicaid prescriptions filled in violation of the CSA.
- The **SUPPORT Act of 2018** (Pub. L. 115-271) tightened pharmacy suspicious order monitoring and expanded the centralized DEA Suspicious Order Reports system.

Source hub: https://www.deadiversion.usdoj.gov/pharmacy.html and https://www.dea.gov/resources/data-and-statistics

## 5. AZ tort and consumer protection frame

**HIGH confidence on statutory citations:**

- **ARS 32-1901 et seq.** AZ Pharmacy Practice Act. Defines "practice of pharmacy" to include "interpreting, evaluating and dispensing prescription orders in the patient's best interests" and "providing patient counseling." Pharmacist-in-charge is responsible to the Board for compliance with state and federal law. Source: https://www.azleg.gov/ars/32/01901.htm
- **ARS 13-3408** Possession, use, administration, acquisition, sale, manufacture, or transportation of narcotic drugs. Subsection A(7): a person shall not knowingly "transport for sale, import into this state, offer to transport for sale or import into this state, sell, transfer or offer to sell or transfer a narcotic drug." Felony classifications run from class 4 (possession) to class 2 (sale, manufacture). Source: https://www.azleg.gov/ars/13/03408.htm
- **ARS 36-2606** Prescription Drug Monitoring Program (CSPMP). Pharmacist duty to query the database before dispensing certain controlled substances. Source URL needs direct retrieval; pharmacypmp.az.gov is the program hub.
- **ARS 44-1521 to 44-1534** Arizona Consumer Fraud Act. ARS 44-1522 prohibits "the act, use or employment by any person of any deception, deceptive or unfair act or practice, fraud, false pretense, false promise, misrepresentation, or concealment, suppression or omission of any material fact with intent that others rely on such concealment, suppression or omission, in connection with the sale or advertisement of any merchandise." Pharmacy services and prescription drugs fall within "merchandise." HIGH confidence on statute, MEDIUM confidence on the pharmacy-services application without an AZ appellate cite locked.
- **ARS 12-611** Wrongful death cause of action. Survives for the benefit of statutorily defined heirs.
- **Federal False Claims Act, 31 U.S.C. 3729.** Qui tam relator path is the standard mechanism for whistleblower-driven pharmacy settlements. Arizona does not have a state-level FCA equivalent at the time of this research; **VERIFY** before publishing.
- **AZ appellate decisions on pharmacy negligent dispensing 2010-2025:** No reported decision was surfaced in this pass. That is itself reportable. The doctrinal anchor is general negligence and the learned-intermediary frame, not a pharmacy-specific AZ appellate holding.
