## 2. The combined frame: which facilities have BOTH billing-fraud citations AND CMS abuse / neglect citations

**The frame, stated precisely (HIGH on the architecture, FLAG on the named overlap list).**

Two enforcement systems run in parallel against the same physical buildings:

1. **AHCCCS Office of Program Integrity + AG Medicaid Fraud Control Unit** investigate billing fraud, including therapy hours billed without documentation, nursing-hour overstatements, and activity-program billing for activities that didn't occur. The primary records are AHCCCS provider suspension notices, MFCU indictments, and AG civil consumer-fraud filings under ARS 44-1521 et seq.
2. **CMS Form 2567 Statement of Deficiencies + AZ DHS Bureau of Long-Term Care Licensing** investigate care quality, including abuse, neglect, pressure ulcers, medication errors, falls, and Immediate Jeopardy events. Primary records are Form 2567 narratives, scope-and-severity grids, and DHS enforcement actions.

**The combined frame: the same facility gets cited under both systems in the same year.** A facility that bills Medicaid for nursing hours that didn't occur is, by definition, the same facility that didn't deliver the nursing care those hours were supposed to fund. The billing fraud and the resident harm are not parallel scandals. They are the same scandal billed twice.

## 7. The Medicaid pipeline: how ghost-hour billing happens

**Payor architecture (HIGH on the structure, MEDIUM on the percentages).** AHCCCS administers Arizona's Medicaid program. Long-term care is administered through ALTCS (Arizona Long Term Care System), which contracts with managed-care organizations to pay nursing facilities a daily per-resident rate plus add-ons for therapy, nursing intensity, and activities. As of January 2025, the single-individual monthly income cap for ALTCS is $2,901. Source: https://www.azahcccs.gov/Members/GetCovered/Categories/ALTCS.html.

**Daily rate structure (FLAG, primary AHCCCS rate sheet not yet pulled).** The ALTCS per-diem rate, the case-mix adjustments, and the ancillary therapy and nursing-hour reimbursement structure are published by AHCCCS in the LTC capitation rate filings. PRR or pull from AHCCCS Rate Setting at https://www.azahcccs.gov.

**How ghost-hour billing happens (HIGH on the mechanism, framework only).**
1. The facility's electronic charting system is supposed to log nursing time, therapy minutes, and activity participation per resident.
2. Facility billing software submits encounter data and Minimum Data Set (MDS) assessments to the managed-care organization, which pays the per-diem.
3. When charted minutes are inflated, when therapy sessions are billed for sessions that didn't occur, or when activities are billed for residents who didn't attend, the facility collects on hours that don't exist. These are "ghost hours."
4. The audit trail is the gap between MDS-coded minutes and the underlying staff timesheet plus the resident chart entry. AZ DHS surveyors and AHCCCS auditors can trip the gap on review.
5. The same gap is, by construction, the gap in actual care delivered. A resident who was billed for two PT sessions but received zero is the same resident the facility didn't get out of bed.

**The combined frame is a billing audit that's also a care audit.** Every dollar of ghost-hour billing is one hour of care that didn't happen to one person. The investigation runs the dollar to the person.

## Person-first language audit

Throughout this dossier and the resulting investigation, "people living in long-term care" replaces "residents alone." "Families whose loved ones live in nursing homes" replaces "victim families." A person harmed by nursing-home neglect is "the person" or "the person living there," not "the victim." A person who died is named with their family's consent before naming, as in the Korman dossier protocol.
