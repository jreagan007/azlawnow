## 3. The settlement terms

**Source for this entire section:** Arizona AG press release, 2026-04-15. https://www.azag.gov/press-release/attorney-general-mayes-secures-7-million-settlement-aps-following-investigation

**Total (HIGH):** $7 million.

**Allocations (HIGH):**
* $2.75 million to the Consumer Protection Consumer Fraud Revolving Fund.
* Up to $250,000 for Attorney General attorneys' fees.
* $1 million to the Arizona Consumer Assistance and Education Program. Of that $1 million, a minimum of $800,000 is required to flow as direct bill credits to eligible APS customers with outstanding balances by September 1, 2026.
* $3.4 million for programmatic improvements and consumer outreach by APS.

Note on the original brief: the brief stated "$1M direct customer credits before September 1, 2026." The AG release language is more precise: $1M to the Consumer Assistance and Education Program, of which a minimum of $800K must be distributed as bill credits before that date. Use the precise framing.

**Reinstated and new APS obligations (HIGH):**
* Reinstate the 95-degree heat hold on residential disconnections outside the existing June 1 to October 15 moratorium window. Disconnections are now temperature-triggered, not calendar-triggered alone.
* Maintain a 32-degree cold-weather hold.
* Add text-message alerts for past-due notices and disconnection notices.
* Enhance the existing APS Safety Net Program for emergency notifications via text and email, particularly for medically vulnerable and at-risk customers.
* Send annual letters to customers comparing the Saver Choice Plus rate plan to alternative rate plans.
* Encourage other Arizona utilities to adopt similar extreme-weather practices.

**Liability admission (HIGH):** None. APS did not admit fault.
Source: KJZZ (Davis-Young), 2026-04-15.

**Statutory anchor (HIGH):** Arizona Consumer Fraud Act, ARS 44-1521 et seq. ARS 44-1522 is the operative unlawful-practices section, prohibiting "deception, deceptive or unfair act or practice, fraud, false pretense, false promise, misrepresentation, or concealment, suppression or omission of any material fact" in connection with the sale or advertisement of merchandise, with intent that others rely on such conduct, "whether or not any person has in fact been misled, deceived or damaged."
Source: ARS 44-1522 statute text at azleg.gov.

**AG's case handler (HIGH):** Senior Litigation Counsel John Raymond Dillion IV, Consumer Protection and Advocacy Section, Civil Litigation Division, Arizona Attorney General's Office.
Source: AG press release.

**Mayes verbatim (HIGH):** "This settlement ensures that APS will no longer disconnect power based on the date on the calendar alone, if temperatures are dangerous, the power stays on."
(Note: the AG release uses an em-dash in the original. AZ Law Now style replaces with comma, as quoted above.)
Source: AG press release.

**Mayes verbatim, second quote (HIGH, KJZZ):** "You shouldn't have to die because the utility disconnects you when it's hot in Arizona."
Source: KJZZ (Davis-Young), 2026-04-15.

## 6. The Consumer Fraud Act frame

**Statute (HIGH):** ARS 44-1521 et seq., Arizona Consumer Fraud Act.

**Operative section (HIGH):** ARS 44-1522, unlawful practices. Quoted text in section 3 above.

**The "unfair or deceptive act" theory of the AG case (MEDIUM, inferred):** The AG's theory, as expressed in the press release and AP reporting, is that APS engaged in an unfair or deceptive act by silently abandoning its voluntary 95-degree heat hold and applying calendar-only disconnect rules during dangerous-heat conditions, while customers reasonably relied on APS's prior representations. The AG release does not publish the complaint text. The settlement is pre-suit or contemporaneous with filing; no sustained litigation record was retrieved. FLAGGED for the precise pleading language.

**Private right of action under the Consumer Fraud Act (HIGH on existence, FLAG on case cite):** Arizona courts recognize an implied private right of action under ARS 44-1522. Sellinger v. Freeway Mobile Home Sales, 110 Ariz. 573 (1974) is the standard cite in AZ practice treatises. Private plaintiffs must show reliance and damages. The state, by contrast, can sue without showing reliance.

## 7. Heat-death context (Maricopa County 2024)

**Source for this section:** Maricopa County Department of Public Health 2024 final heat-related deaths report, posted preliminarily 2025-03-10 and finalized 2025-05-22. URL: https://www.maricopa.gov/CivicAlerts.asp?AID=3222

**2024 confirmed heat-related deaths (HIGH):** 602 confirmed, with 8 still under investigation at report finalization. First year-over-year decrease since 2014. Down from 645 in 2023.

**Indoor death AC status (HIGH):** Among indoor heat-related deaths, 88 percent had an AC unit present. Of those with AC present, 70 percent of units were nonfunctional.

**Utility disconnect prior 30 days (FLAG):** The MCDPH report does not break out or publish utility-disconnect status of decedents. FLAGGED. Recommend direct outreach to MCDPH heat surveillance lead or FOIA for non-public underlying records.

**Outdoor share (HIGH):** More than half of 2024 heat-related deaths occurred outdoors, with 49 percent of decedents experiencing homelessness at time of death.

**2024 heat severity context (HIGH):** Phoenix recorded 113 consecutive days at or above 100 degrees in 2024. 51 percent of heat-related deaths occurred on days when heat risk was calculated as moderate, not extreme.
