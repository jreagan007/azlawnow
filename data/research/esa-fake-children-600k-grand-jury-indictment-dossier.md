## EXECUTIVE VERIFICATION SUMMARY

**Anchor figure ($600,000+) is HIGH confidence.** AG Mayes named the loss as "more than $600,000" in the State Grand Jury indictment announced February 29, 2024. Source: https://www.azag.gov/press-release/attorney-general-mayes-announces-indictments-esa-program

**Indictment date: February 29, 2024.** HIGH confidence. The proposal text loosely treats this as a fresh 2026 story. It isn't. The indictment is two years old. The publishable angle is the trail since: arraignments, additional indictments (December 2024 round), the $1.2M ADE recovery referenced on Arizona PBS Horizon February 18, 2026, the August 2025 conviction of Bowers/Hewitt in a separate ESA case, and the May 2026 Luminaria reporting on dueling reform ballot initiatives. The "fake children" hook stays alive because the criminal case is still moving and the structural fix isn't.

**The five named defendants:** HIGH confidence. Dolores Lashay Sweet, Dorrian Lamarr Jones, Jennifer Lopez (all former AZED ESA program specialists), plus Jadakah Celeste Johnson and Raymond Lamont Johnson, Jr. (Sweet's adult children, alleged conduits). Source: AG press release plus AZ Mirror, KTAR, ABC15 contemporaneous reporting.

**17 children, 5 of them fabricated, forged disability evaluations on every one:** HIGH confidence on the topline. Mayes stated verbatim: "they created ghost children with forged birth certificates, children that didn't exist, and gave them fake disability diagnoses that would make them eligible for larger funding amounts." 12 News reported "17 children were used in the scheme and five of them were completely fake." Sweet allegedly claimed non-existent triplets at her address; Lopez allegedly claimed non-existent twins; all five fictitious children shared the same birthday. The forged-document categories named by AG: birth certificates and falsified special education evaluations. The proposal's claim that "every one" of the 17 had forged disability docs is FLAG. The press release language is "false, forged or fraudulent documentation" used to admit "real and fictitious" students. Don't write "every one" without pulling the indictment counts. Better framing: "fake disability evaluations were a core part of the scheme" or "across the 17 children."

**Detection: not by AZED, despite Horne's framing.** Horne's office referred Jones and Lopez to the AG. A credit union flagged Sweet's account activity to the AG separately. The AZED audit function did not catch the scheme on its own across 26 months of monthly disbursements. HIGH confidence. Sources: AZED communications page, AZ Mirror reporting.

**Time period of fraud: October 2021 through November 2023.** HIGH confidence. AZ Mirror reporting from the AG's filing.

## 5. AZED oversight failures

What the public record supports:

1. **The fraud ran 26 months (Oct 2021 to Nov 2023) without internal AZED detection.** HIGH. Time-period source: AZ Mirror.

2. **AZED did not catch the Sweet ring on its own.** HIGH. Mayes' office described detection by a credit union flagging Sweet's account activity. Horne's office separately referred Jones and Lopez. So even on Horne's own account, the inside-employee AZED ring was caught by an external bank, not by AZED's own ESA financial controls.

3. **The defendants had application-approval authority.** HIGH. The AG release says they "approved ESA applications for minor students, both real and fictitious, and admitted them into the program by using false, forged or fraudulent documentation." This is the structural failure. The same employees who could create child profiles could approve the disability evaluations that doubled or tripled the per-child payout.

4. **Horne's defensive posture: he hired a 19-year veteran of the Auditor General's Office to oversee ESA financial structure and a full-time investigator with law-enforcement experience after taking office in January 2023.** MEDIUM (sourced via AZED communications statement, not independently corroborated). Worth noting that all the indicted conduct overlaps with Horne's tenure (Jan 2023 onward through Nov 2023).

5. **AZED referred seven instances of suspected ESA fraud to the AG in 2024 alone.** MEDIUM (AZED communications statement).

6. **Arizona PBS Horizon Feb 18, 2026 reporting:** Of approximately 385,000 ESA purchases reviewed, about 84,000 (~22%) were deemed unallowable. AZED has recovered about $1.2 million through collections, repayments, or referrals. Prohibited items flagged include diamond rings, gift cards, and luxury clothing. HIGH (broadcast segment).

7. **Audit-law compliance failure.** AZ Mirror reported July 29, 2025 that the ESA program "ignored state audit law for nearly a year." That's a structural-controls story tied to the same program but not to these five defendants. FLAG for follow-up; if it survives a primary-source pull (Auditor General report or AZED admission), it strengthens the failure-of-controls section.

The structural problem the case exposes:

- An ESA specialist with both application-creation and approval authority can create a fictitious child, attach a falsified disability evaluation, and collect quarterly multipliers from a state ESA account with no independent counter-verification of the child's existence at any step in the workflow. The Sweet ring is the proof case. The fix is segregation of duties and an independent identity-verification layer. Neither is in current AZED ESA controls as documented.
