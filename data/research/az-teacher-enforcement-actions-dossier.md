## Headline correction (verify before write)

The proposal hook said "200+ enforcement actions in 2024." The verified primary source figure is **325 actions adjudicated by the Arizona State Board of Education in calendar year 2024.** The Board itself had projected 240. Actual outpaced projection by 85 cases.

The proposal also said "sexual misconduct tops the list." This is **partly correct and needs reframing.** The cumulative 2012 to 2024 picture has sexual misconduct as the #1 category (36%). But in **2024 alone**, the largest category was assault, non sexual (45%), with sexual misconduct second at 29%. The Board's own report notes it "prioritizes investigation of sexual misconduct cases," meaning that category appears earlier in the adjudication queue. The story should lead with the cumulative 36% figure plus the 2024 mix, not a false 2024 only ranking.

## 6. Statutory and procedural framework (HIGH confidence)

### Authority

- **ARS 15.203(A)(14) and (A)(20).** Board authority to impose discipline on certificated and non certificated educators for immoral or unprofessional conduct. https://www.azleg.gov/ars/15/00203.htm
- **HB2023 (Laws 2021, Ch. 2).** Extended Board jurisdiction to non certificated school personnel.
- **AAC R7.2.1301 et seq.** (especially R7.2.1308 defining immoral / unprofessional conduct). Procedural rules. https://apps.azsos.gov/public_services/Title_07/7-02.pdf
- **ARS 15.505, 15.534, 15.534.02, 15.534.04, 15.550.** Supplementary authority chains for arrests, convictions, out of state revocations, and certificate validity.
- **ARS 15.514.** Mandatory reporting by school personnel of educator misconduct to the Board's Investigative Unit.
- **ARS 13.3620.** Arizona's mandatory child abuse reporter statute.

### Process

1. Allegation reported to Investigative Unit (IU)
2. Parallel IU investigation alongside law enforcement
3. Negotiated Settlement Agreement option (NSAs are ~20% of adjudications, can range from Letter of Censure to 5 year suspension)
4. If no NSA, hearing before Professional Practices Advisory Committee (PPAC). Three PPAC panels, 9 members each
5. PPAC issues findings of fact, conclusions of law, recommendation
6. Full Board votes. Adopt, alter, or dismiss
7. Compliance monitoring by Board staff

### Action categories

- Voluntary surrender (most common in 2024)
- Suspension (up to 5 years)
- Suspension with conditions
- Revocation (permanent)
- Letter of censure (public reprimand, no certificate loss)
- Denial of certification application
- Dismissal (no action)

### "Do not employ" mechanism

Surrender, suspension, or revocation status combined with the Fingerprint Clearance Card requirement under HB2023 functions as a do not employ list. Public lookup: https://azsbe.az.gov/educator-discipline/discipline-search

## 7. NASDTEC Clearinghouse. Cross state status (MEDIUM confidence)

- Arizona is a NASDTEC jurisdiction member. Final public misconduct dispositions are reported to the national Clearinghouse. https://www.nasdtec.net/
- The Board's own report shows 6% of cumulative cases (4% in 2024) were sourced via NASDTEC database monitoring. This confirms Arizona uses the Clearinghouse to catch educators with out of state revocations.
- **Gap:** Public records do not confirm whether ADE or AZ school districts subscribe as Associate Members for proactive search and alert features. This means an Arizona district hiring a teacher revoked in another state may not catch it unless the candidate self discloses or DPS finds it in fingerprinting.
- USA Today's 2016 "Broken Discipline Tracking System" investigation flagged Arizona in its national findings. The Board's 2024 report does not directly cite USA Today but its NASDTEC inclusion responds to the same concerns.

**Confidence MEDIUM.** Arizona membership confirmed. Associate Member subscription status by individual districts is not in primary sources surfaced.

## 8. Tort theory for survivor side civil claims (HIGH confidence)

### Statutes

- **ARS 12.514.** Civil action for childhood sexual abuse must be commenced within 12 years after the plaintiff reaches age 18 (so up to age 30). The 2019 expansion (HB2466) extended limits and added a discovery rule. Covers both direct sexual conduct / contact and failure to report under ARS 13.3620. https://www.azleg.gov/ars/12/00514.htm
- **ARS 12.821.01.** 180 day Notice of Claim requirement against public school districts. Failure to file bars the suit. https://www.azleg.gov/ars/12/08210.htm
- **ARS 15.341.** Districts' duty to maintain a safe school environment.
- **ARS 15.514.** Mandatory reporting of educator misconduct. Failure exposes individuals and districts to civil liability.
- **Title IX (20 USC 1681 et seq.).** Private right of action against K to 12 districts for "deliberate indifference" to known harassment or abuse.

### Claim theories against Arizona school districts

| Theory | Required element | Arizona statutory anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Negligent hiring | Failure to check backgrounds or hired despite known risks | ARS 15.341 + common law |
| Negligent supervision | Inadequate oversight allowing abuse | ARS 15.514 + common law |
| Negligent retention | Kept abuser employed after complaints | Common law + Title IX deliberate indifference |
| Failure to report | Mandatory reporter duty breached | ARS 15.514 / 13.3620 |
| Title IX deliberate indifference | District knew, did nothing | 20 USC 1681 |
| Direct vicarious liability | Acts within scope of employment | Restatement of Agency |

### Procedural realities

- 180 day Notice of Claim clock under ARS 12.821.01 is the critical near term deadline once a claim accrues against a district.
- ARS 12.514 gives survivors discovered trauma flexibility but does NOT extend the 180 day notice clock against public entities.
- These two timelines often conflict and require careful sequencing. A survivor side firm's job is to triage when a Notice of Claim must be filed against a district vs. when the longer 12 year clock controls civil action against the educator individually.

**Recent Arizona appellate cases on district liability for educator misconduct:** No specific named appellate ruling surfaced in this research pass. The Perplexity returns acknowledged the gap. Trial court settlements and federal Title IX claims dominate the volume. Confidence on this gap: HIGH (gap confirmed across two queries).

## 14. Five strongest HIGH confidence facts (lead with these)

1. **Arizona disciplined 325 educators in 2024.** A 491% increase from the 55 actions in 2012, and 19% above 2023's caseload. Source: AZ State Board of Education 2024 Enforcement Action Report, p.3.

2. **Sexual misconduct is the largest category in the 13 year record (36% of 1,876 cases since 2012)**, even though 2024 alone was led by non sexual assault (45%) with sexual misconduct second at 29%. The Board explicitly prioritizes investigation of sexual misconduct cases. Source: same report, p.16, Exhibit 11.

3. **Maricopa County is where the discipline pipeline runs.** 332 of the 597 educators disciplined Jan 2023 to Dec 2024 last worked in Maricopa, more than every other county combined. Source: 2024 EA Report Exhibit 14, p.20.

4. **Male educators are disciplined at roughly 5x the rate of female educators.** 0.02872 vs. 0.00579 per certificate holder. Source: 2024 EA Report Exhibit 8, p.11.

5. **Voluntary surrender is the dominant outcome.** 734 of 1,876 cumulative cases (39%) ended in surrender, and 2024 set a new annual high. Survivors and families should know this before being told an educator "voluntarily resigned." That may be a Board surrender that ends Arizona certification. Source: 2024 EA Report Exhibit 15, p.20.

## 15. Story angles for Brendan Franks voice

### Lead candidate
The Arizona State Board of Education adjudicated 325 misconduct cases against educators in 2024. That's not a forecast. That's the Board's own count, in the report it published in May. Sexual misconduct accounted for 36% of every case since 2012. In 2024, 94 of those 325 actions involved sexual misconduct allegations. The Board says it prioritizes those cases first. Maricopa County alone produced more than half the discipline pipeline.

### Survivor first angle (the Brendan Franks frame)
The 325 figure tells us how many educators the system caught. It does not tell us how many survivors are still inside the 12 year statute of limitations under ARS 12.514, still inside the 180 day Notice of Claim window under ARS 12.821.01 against a public district, or still trying to figure out whether what happened to their child years ago has a legal remedy today. That's the conversation the data should start.

### Accountability angle
Voluntary surrender, not revocation, is now the most common outcome. Surrender ends Arizona certification, but it does not produce the same public hearing and findings of fact as a revocation. Families considering whether to file a Notice of Claim against a district need to know that an educator who "resigned" may have actually surrendered to the Board. The discipline search portal at azsbe.az.gov/educator-discipline/discipline-search is the public mechanism to verify.

### Headline alternatives to test
- "Arizona Disciplined 325 Educators in 2024. Here's What the State Board's Own Report Doesn't Lead With."
- "What 13 Years of Arizona Educator Discipline Data Tells Survivor Families About Their Window to Act."
- "The 491% Increase the Arizona State Board Won't Take Credit For."
